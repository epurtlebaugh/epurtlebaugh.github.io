What is it?

üßâ

Yerba mate is a traditional South American drink made from the leaves of the *Ilex paraguariensis* plant. It's popular in countries like Argentina, Uruguay, and Brazil. The leaves are dried, crushed, and steeped in hot water.

How do you drink it?

üßâ

It's traditionally enjoyed using a hollowed-out gourd called a *mate*. Dried yerba mate leaves are placed in the gourd, and hot (but not boiling) water is poured over them. The drinker sips the infusion through a metal straw, or *bombilla*.

How does it taste?

üßâ

It has a distinct, earthy flavor with a slight bitterness and a hint of herbal notes. Some describe it as having a grassy or vegetal taste. Some version are smoked, giving a rich depth to the flavor even more so.






Who Enjoyed It First?

Yerba mate, a traditional South American beverage made from the leaves of the Ilex paraguariensis plant, has a rich history rooted in indigenous cultures. The Guaran√≠ people, native to the regions of present-day Paraguay, Argentina, and Brazil, were the first to cultivate and consume yerba mate. They brewed it in a hollowed-out gourd and shared it socially, a practice that symbolized friendship and community.

Introduction to the World

The Spanish colonizers, who arrived in South America in the 16th century, encountered yerba mate and soon became intrigued by its stimulating properties. They initially adopted the beverage, integrating it into their own social practices. Over time, yerba mate cultivation expanded, and European settlers began to recognize its commercial potential.

Modern Day

In the 19th and 20th centuries, yerba mate gained wider recognition beyond South America. The beverage became popular in countries like Argentina, Brazil, Uruguay, and Paraguay, where it remains a staple of daily life. Its global presence grew with the rise of international trade and cultural exchange, leading to its introduction to new markets. Today, yerba mate is enjoyed worldwide.
